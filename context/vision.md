# Vision: Compass

Compass exists because the artifact of planning is worth less than the act. A
plan at its tip is a disposable guess about work not yet done; the reasoning
that moved it from the last guess to this one is what compounds. Tools that
store the plan and discard the reasoning have it backwards.

Compass succeeds when an agent or human arriving cold can read *why the plan is
what it is* — not just what it currently says — and when that record survives
being wrong. It succeeds when revising a plan is cheap and lossless, when two
machines planning at once produce a visible disagreement rather than a silent
loss, and when nothing about the tool tempts anyone to keep plans somewhere else
instead.

It fails if it becomes an issue tracker.
